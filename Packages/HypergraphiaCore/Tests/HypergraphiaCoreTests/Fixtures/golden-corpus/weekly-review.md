# Weekly Review Template

Sunday evening, 30 minutes. Most of what makes my week go well or badly happens in this slot.

## Look back

- What did I actually finish this week (not "worked on" — finished)?
- What slipped, and why? Was it scope, distraction, energy, or wrong-priority?
- Any meetings I should have skipped? (Often: yes.)
- Any conversation I owe a follow-up on?

## Clear the inboxes

Email to zero, but not all of those need replies — most get archived or deferred. Read-later list: triage, not consume. Voice memos / scratch notes / meeting captures: file or trash, don't leave them floating.

## Look forward

Three outcomes I want by next Sunday. Not tasks — outcomes. "Ship X" instead of "work on X." If I can't name three, the upcoming week doesn't have a shape and I'll just react to whatever lands in my inbox.

## Calendar audit

Walk through next week's meetings. For each one, can I articulate the outcome the meeting needs to produce? If not, the agenda needs work or the meeting shouldn't happen. Block deep-work time before someone else does — every recurring 30 minutes of someone else's process eats more than its weight on the days it lands.

## What I cut

I tried adding a "review what I'm grateful for" block. Felt forced; the morning journal already does that. Tried adding a quarterly section. The quarterly view belongs in a separate quarterly review, not stacked here.

# Morning Routine

What works for me, when I actually do it.

## Before the phone

The first 30 minutes have to happen without checking email, messages, or social media. The decision quality of the rest of the day collapses if I let inputs in before I'm settled. This is the only rule that matters; everything else is variation.

## The actual sequence

1. Glass of water on the nightstand, drink before standing up.
2. Make the bed. Boring but the visual signal that the day has started.
3. Coffee — black, while the kettle boils I do five minutes of mobility work (cat-cow, hip openers, shoulder dislocates with a band).
4. Sit somewhere with the coffee and journal for ten minutes. Three things from yesterday, three intentions for today, one thing I'm grateful for. If I skip this and jump to email, the day always feels reactive instead of intentional.
5. Plan the deep-work block for the morning. One task only. If I list more than one, the plan was actually a wishlist.

## What I cut

I tried meditation in the mornings for years. It works for some people but for me it competed with the journal — they're both "process the inside of your head" tasks and stacking them made both worse. Picked the journal because it produces an artifact I can re-read.

## When it goes wrong

Travel days break the routine. Don't fight that. Pick the one piece that travels best (for me: the journal, which works on paper or in any text editor) and let the rest go for a few days.

# Coffee Brewing

How I make coffee at home, after several embarrassing pour-over phases.

## Beans

Whole beans, roasted within the last three weeks. Specialty roasters publish a roast date; supermarket bags don't. The single biggest variable is freshness — bad beans freshly roasted beat great beans three months old.

## Grind

A burr grinder is non-negotiable. Blade grinders produce uneven particles which extract unevenly. A Baratza Encore for $150 is the floor; everything above that is marginal returns until you're spending $700+.

## Method

V60 pour-over for solo mornings, French press for two cups, Aeropress when I'm traveling. Espresso requires a separate grinder and a separate set of opinions; I gave up trying to do espresso at home well.

## Ratios

V60: 22g coffee, 360g water, 3-minute pour. French press: 30g coffee, 500g water, 4 minutes, plunge slowly. Both use water just off boil — about 200°F.

## What I cut

The third pour-over device. The fourth grinder upgrade. The "blooming" debate (yes, do it, but it's not the difference between good and bad coffee).

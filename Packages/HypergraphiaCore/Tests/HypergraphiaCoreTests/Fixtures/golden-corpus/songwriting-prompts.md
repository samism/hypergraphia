# Songwriting Prompts

When I'm stuck staring at a blank page.

- Steal a chord progression from a song you love and write entirely new melody and lyrics over it. Most great songwriters did this on purpose for years before anyone noticed.
- Set a timer for fifteen minutes and write a verse. Don't edit. The point isn't a finished song; it's exit velocity.
- Borrow a structural constraint from poetry — a sonnet's rhyme scheme, a haiku's syllable count, a sestina's repeated end-words.
- Write the worst song you can, on purpose, in a deliberately bad genre. Then keep three lines.
- Pick a single image (a phone booth in the rain, a sleeping dog) and circle around it for the whole song without ever stating the image directly.

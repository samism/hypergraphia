# Guitar Practice

What 30 minutes a day looks like when I'm being honest.

## The structure

Five minutes warmup (chromatic exercises, slow). Ten minutes technique (whatever I'm currently bad at — usually alternate picking on the high strings). Ten minutes repertoire. Five minutes "play a song badly and have fun."

## Technique focus

Right now: economy picking on string crossings. The trick is making the picking-hand motion smaller, not faster. Speed is a side effect of relaxation. Tense fingers hit a wall at maybe 120 BPM; relaxed fingers go past 180 without noticing.

## Repertoire

I rotate three pieces at a time. One from sight-reading practice (currently a Bach minuet), one I'm memorizing (Wes Montgomery's "Four on Six"), one comfort piece I've already learned (a chord-melody arrangement of "Body and Soul"). When the memorized one is solid, it becomes the new comfort piece and I start something new.

## Recording yourself

The most useful tool. Phone, single take, no fixing in post. The version of me that listens back is much more honest than the version of me playing. Tempo is steady or it isn't; the chord change is clean or it isn't. No amount of "I felt it was good" survives the playback.

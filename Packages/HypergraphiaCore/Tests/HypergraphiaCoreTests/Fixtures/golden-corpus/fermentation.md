# Fermentation Basics

Notes from a year of experimenting.

## The two camps

Lacto-fermentation (vegetables, hot sauce, sauerkraut, kimchi) and yeast-fermentation (bread, beer, wine, kombucha). Different microbes, different sugars consumed, different end products. Lacto produces lactic acid; yeast produces CO2 and ethanol. Same kitchen, completely different rules.

## Salt is the gatekeeper

For vegetables: 2-3% salt by weight of vegetables. Below that and undesirable bacteria win. Above 5% and lactobacillus is too suppressed to do its job. Weigh, don't eyeball — eye-balling is how you get mushy or moldy ferments.

## Anaerobic vs. aerobic

Lacto-fermentation needs the vegetables submerged under brine. Mold grows on the surface where oxygen reaches; the fermentation happens below the line. A weight (glass, ceramic, water-filled bag) is the cheap fix. An airlock lid is the upgrade.

## Time and temperature

Cooler is slower and more nuanced; warmer is faster and more one-note. 65-70°F is the sweet spot for most vegetable ferments. Sauerkraut at 65°F over three weeks beats sauerkraut at 80°F over five days every time. Refrigerate when it's where you want it; cold doesn't stop fermentation but it slows it to a crawl.

# Deep Work Habits

What actually moves the needle vs. what feels productive.

## Morning is non-negotiable

Two protected hours from the moment I sit down. No Slack, no email, no "just a quick check." I keep the laptop in airplane mode and only browser tabs open are the ones I need for the task. Once I've broken the morning block, I rarely get it back the same day.

## One task per block

If I list two, the plan was a wishlist and I'll context-switch enough to ruin both. The list is one specific outcome, not a category. "Finish the auth refactor" not "work on auth."

## Friction with collaborators

Async-first. A response within a day is fine for almost everything. The expectation that a Slack message gets a response within minutes is the worst productivity tax in modern knowledge work, and most of the people complaining about it created the expectation themselves.

## When to give up

If I've spent two hours stuck on the same problem and the docs/codebase aren't yielding, change something. Walk. Whiteboard. Ask someone. Stuck in the same loop for the third time means the approach is wrong, not that I need to push harder.

## Related

The morning routine and weekly review are the load-bearing rituals. Deep work without the routine is a coin flip.

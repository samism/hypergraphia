# Sourdough

The method I keep coming back to.

## Starter

Equal parts flour and water by weight. Feed once a day at room temperature; if the kitchen is cold, twice a day. A ripe starter doubles within 4-6 hours after feeding and smells tangy but not vinegar-sharp. Wild yeast is patient — don't rush it.

## Dough

Roughly 70% hydration for sandwich loaves, 78% for open-crumb boules. Mix flour, water, salt, and ~20% starter. Stretch and fold every 30 minutes for the first two hours, then bulk-rise until the dough rises 50%. Shape, cold proof in the fridge overnight (8-16 hours), bake in a Dutch oven at 500°F for 20 minutes covered, 425°F for 25 minutes uncovered.

## Common failures

Dense crumb usually means underproofed bulk. Gummy interior means underbaked — internal temp should hit 205°F. Flat boules means weak shaping or overproofed. The crust caramelizing too dark before the inside is done means the oven is too hot for your loaf size; drop 25°F and add 5 minutes.

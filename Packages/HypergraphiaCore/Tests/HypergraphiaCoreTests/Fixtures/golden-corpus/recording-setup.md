# Home Recording Setup

Logic Pro on a closet-sized desk. Apollo Twin X interface, one Shure SM7B for vocals, one Sennheiser e609 on the guitar amp, one pair of small-diaphragm condensers as room mics for acoustic guitar.

The room is treated, not soundproofed — there's a difference. Treatment means absorption panels in the corners and a bass trap behind the desk; the room sounds dead enough not to fight the recording. Soundproofing would mean keeping outside sound out, which I gave up on once a leaf-blower convinced me the project was unwinnable.

The most important upgrade I've made: not buying more gear. Every time I felt the urge to add a preamp or compressor, the better answer was to spend that money on a recording lesson with someone who'd been doing this for twenty years.

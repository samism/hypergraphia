# Kettlebell Programming

What I've kept consistent over the last year.

## The four lifts

Swing, clean, snatch, get-up. Everything else is variation. Swings build the hinge pattern and posterior chain. Cleans bridge to the rack position. Snatches are the cardio engine. Turkish get-ups are the diagnostic — if a get-up feels bad, something else is wrong.

## Weekly structure

Three days a week is plenty if intensity is right. Day 1: heavy swings (5x10), light cleans (5x5/side), short get-ups. Day 2: moderate snatches in a 10-minute EMOM, presses for accessory. Day 3: long set of swings (10x10 with short rest), get-ups as a cooldown.

## Bell selection

A 24kg (men) or 16kg (women) is the workhorse for most people. Heavier than that is for advanced lifters; lighter than that is fine for technique work but won't drive much adaptation. Don't pick weights based on ego — pick based on what lets you keep good form for the work set length.

## Common mistakes

Hyperextending the lower back at the top of swings (the bell goes UP because your hips drove it, not because your arms lifted it). Leaning forward in the rack position. Skipping mobility work — kettlebell training rewards thoracic mobility and ankle dorsiflexion more than people expect.
